# Cold Review Prompt: Agentic PM Skill Package

Paste this prompt into the reviewing LLM. Attach the seven SKILL.md files. Do NOT attach the books, the framework catalog, or any background material.

---

You are reviewing a Claude Cowork skill package designed for senior product managers building agentic AI products. The package has seven skills: a router (`agentic-pm-lifecycle`), five lifecycle phase skills (`agentic-pm-discover-decide`, `agentic-pm-design`, `agentic-pm-eval`, `agentic-pm-observe`, `agentic-pm-operate`), and one portfolio-strategy skill (`agentic-pm-behavior-governance`).

The audience: senior PMs (10 plus years experience) who may be new to agentic AI but are fluent in classical product management. Each skill is invoked at a moment of need ("I am writing a go-or-no-go memo," "I am designing the approval moment") and produces structured artifacts. Each skill has a "first-timer foundations" section that teaches constructs the PM has not seen before.

The skills reference four books by the same author but you have not read them. The package's stated premise is that the skills should be usable at the moment of invocation, with the books as optional deepening references. Test that premise.

YOUR JOB:

Read all seven SKILL.md files. Imagine you are a senior PM who has not read the books, invoking each skill at a real moment of need. Report the following, ordered by leverage (highest impact first):

1. Where does the skill assume context the PM cannot supply. Be specific. Name the section and the missing context.

2. Where does jargon survive without definition. Identify terms that appear without enough teaching for a first-timer to act on them.

3. Where does an artifact's output format produce a result the PM would struggle to interpret or hand to a stakeholder.

4. Where does the voice or framing ring false, overstate, or use rhetorical moves that only work if the reader already agrees with the underlying frame.

5. Where do the skills contradict each other or duplicate each other in ways that would confuse a PM moving between phases.

6. What is missing entirely. A senior PM at a moment of need would expect to find X in this package and X is not there.

7. Specific edits you would make. Quote the original sentence and propose the replacement. Maximum 10 such edits.

DO NOT report:

Praise. The author already knows what is strong. Skip the "this is a thoughtful framework" preamble.

Generic LLM observations like "consider adding more examples" or "could benefit from clearer headers." Be specific or say nothing.

Style preferences. The author has a defined voice (direct, evidence-anchored, adversarial to weak answers, no em dashes). Do not suggest softening.

Suggestions to add cross-references to the books. The author already manages book references at the close of each skill. Focus on what the skill itself is missing, not on what could be deferred to a book.

Length cap: 1,500 words. Be direct and specific. The author wants to know where this breaks for a first-timer PM, not what it does well.
